§ 01 / Premise

An energy transition is only regenerative if the people and places hosting it come out stronger than they went in.

The clean energy buildout is the largest infrastructure project of our lifetime. Most of it is being done to communities — sited there, financed elsewhere, staffed from elsewhere, with the long-term returns flowing somewhere else.

A regenerative energy community works differently. The system is sized to local need. The workforce is trained and retained locally. The economics are structured so that value stays in the place that grew them.

Whether that's actually buildable — and what it would take to make the first one real — is the question Regen Comm exists to answer.

§ 02 / Current Focus

Three threads, one fabric.

i.

Energy Systems

Grid-scale, community-scale, resilience hubs, behind the meter.

How do solar and storage get designed across every scale they touch — from the larger grid down to community projects, resilience hubs, and privately owned systems? Each scale answers a different question, and a regenerative buildout has to work at all of them.

ii.

Community Development

Ownership, governance, wealth that stays.

What does it take for a solar project to actually develop the community it's in — instead of extracting from it? Co-ops, land trusts, municipal utilities, anchor partnerships: which structures hold up, and what does it take to organize them?

iii.

Workforce Development

Solar careers, built locally.

The industry needs hundreds of thousands of new workers. The question is whether those jobs become career pathways with real wages, real ladders, and real local hiring — or another wave of itinerant contract labor that leaves no trace.

§ 03 / Research

Published & in progress.

01
Regenerative Energy Communities: Considering PV Manufacturing
Uses the regenerative energy community framework to examine how U.S. PV-manufacturing grants and energy-transition funding might be evaluated together — a first step toward a regeneration-assessment model for large government programs.
In progress Working paper.
Drafting
02
Regenerative Energy Community Modeling Considering Distribution Network Resilience
A modeling approach for regenerative energy communities that explicitly accounts for the resilience of the distribution network they're embedded in.
IEEE EPEC · 2025 With Santiago Grijalva. Pages 305–309.
Published
03
The Role of Electricity Prosumers in Regenerative Communities
A framework for how community prosumers — consumers with their own managed solar PV — can use local generation to support community resilience and reinvestment, not just personal bill savings.
IEEE PES ISGT · 2024 With Santiago Grijalva. Pages 1–5.
Published

§ 05 / On the Horizon

Agrivoltaics — where solar and the land share a season.

Once the community and workforce questions are on firmer ground, the next thread of the research is agrivoltaics: solar arrays designed to coexist productively with farming and grazing on the same land.

It's an open question we're following — and one we think the community-ownership and workforce frameworks will eventually need to absorb.

§ 06 / The Lab

AW LABS LLC works at the edge of energy and the living world.

AW Labs is a small research studio thinking about how the energy transition can be designed to actually develop the communities it touches — not just power them.

Regen Comm is the studio's current focus. Right now that work looks like writing, reading, and showing up — building the foundation of relationships and evidence that any real project will have to stand on.

If your community or your organization is part of this question, we'd like to hear from you.
